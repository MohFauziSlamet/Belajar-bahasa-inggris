
| Question | Translation | Answer |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **1. What's her last name?** | Apa nama belakangnya? | Anderson. |
| **2. What's her first name?** | Apa nama depannya? | Keesha. |
| **3. Where's she from?** | Dari mana asalnya? | The United States. |
| **4. What's her job?** | Apa pekerjaannya? | She's a journalist. |

| **6. What's her phone number?** | Apa nomor teleponnya? | (310) 440-7305. |
| **7. How old is she?** | Berapa umurnya? | Twenty-eight. |
| **8. Is she married?** | Apakah dia menikah? | No, she isn't. |

#### 💡 Key Question Words to Remember:
- **What's** = What is (untuk bertanya tentang nama, pekerjaan, alamat)
- **Where's** = Where is (untuk bertanya tentang tempat/asal)
- **How old** = Berapa umur
- **Is she** = Apakah dia

> [!NOTE]
> **Wh- Questions vs Yes/No Questions:**
> - **Wh- Questions** (What, Where) ask for information. *(Pertanyaan Wh- meminta informasi.)*
> - **Yes/No Questions** (Is she...?) ask for a "Yes" or "No" answer. *(Pertanyaan Ya/Tidak meminta jawaban "Ya" atau "Tidak".)*

### 💡 Extra Examples
- **Q:** What's his job? *(Apa pekerjaannya?)*
- **A:** He's a teacher. *(Dia seorang guru.)*
- **Q:** Is he a teacher? *(Apakah dia seorang guru?)*
- **A:** Yes, he is. *(Ya, benar.)*

## PART 2: Grammar Spot - Yes/No Questions
### ✅ Pertanyaan Ya/Tidak

### Forming Yes/No Questions with "To Be"

**Question Pattern:**
```
Is/Are + Subject + ...?
```

> [!TIP]
> **Intonation:** When asking Yes/No questions, your voice usually goes **up** at the end.
> *(Intonasi: Saat bertanya pertanyaan Ya/Tidak, suaramu biasanya **naik** di akhir kalimat.)*
> - *Is she married? ⤴️*

**Examples:**

| Question | Answer | Translation |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Is she English? | No, she isn't. | Apakah dia orang Inggris? |
| Is she French? | No, she isn't. | Apakah dia orang Prancis? |
| Is she American? | Yes, she is. | Apakah dia orang Amerika? |

### Short Answers (Jawaban Singkat)

#### ✅ Positive:
- **Yes, she is.** *(Ya, dia adalah)*
- **Yes, it is.** *(Ya, itu adalah)*
- **Yes, I am.** *(Ya, saya adalah)*

#### ❌ Negative:
- **No, she isn't.** *(Tidak, dia bukan)*
- **No, it isn't.** *(Tidak, itu bukan)*
- **No, I'm not.** *(Tidak, saya bukan)*

### Negative Forms (Bentuk Negatif)

| Full Form | Contracted Form | Translation |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| I am not | I'm not | Saya bukan |
| You are not | You aren't | Kamu bukan |
| He is not | He isn't | Dia (laki-laki) bukan |
| She is not | She isn't | Dia (perempuan) bukan |
| It is not | It isn't | Itu bukan |
| We are not | We aren't | Kami bukan |
| They are not | They aren't | Mereka bukan |

> ⚠️ **NOTE:** "I amn't" is **INCORRECT!** Always use **"I'm not"**

### Grammar Spot: Possessive 'S (Kepemilikan 'S)

#### Two uses of **'s**:

#### 1️⃣ Contraction (She's = She is):
```
She's a teacher.     → Dia adalah seorang guru.
He's from Japan.     → Dia dari Jepang.
```

#### 2️⃣ Possession (Kepemilikan):
```
Patrick's wife = his wife       → Istri Patrick = istrinya
Brenda's job = her job          → Pekerjaan Brenda = pekerjaannya
```

#### Examples:
**Q:** Who's Brenda? *(Siapa Brenda?)*
**A:** She's Patrick's wife. *(Dia adalah istri Patrick.)*

**Q:** What's her name? *(Siapa namanya?)*
**A:** Her name's Laura. *(Namanya Laura.)*

> [!CAUTION]
> **Common Mistake:** Don't confuse **'s** (is) and **'s** (possession).
> - She**'s** a teacher. (She is) -> *Dia adalah seorang guru.*
> - Patrick**'s** wife. (Possession) -> *Istri Patrick (bukan "Patrick adalah istri").*

## PART 4: Verb "To Be" - Practice
### 🎯 Latihan Verb "To Be"

> [!TIP]
> **Speaking vs Writing:**
> - **Speaking:** Use contractions (I'm, You're). *(Berbicara: Gunakan singkatan.)*
> - **Formal Writing:** Use full forms (I am, You are). *(Menulis Formal: Gunakan bentuk lengkap.)*

Complete the sentences with the correct form of "to be":
*(Lengkapi kalimat dengan bentuk yang benar dari "to be")*

1. **I'm not** at home.
   *(Saya tidak di rumah.)*

2. We **are** in class.
   *(Kami di kelas.)*

3. It **is** Monday today.
   *(Hari ini hari Senin.)*

4. My teacher's name **is** John.
   *(Nama guru saya adalah John.)*

5. My mother and father **are** at work.
   *(Ibu dan ayah saya di tempat kerja.)*

6. I **am/am not** married.
   *(Saya menikah/tidak menikah.)*

7. My grandmother **is** seventy-five years old.
   *(Nenek saya berumur 75 tahun.)*

8. Marcos and Carlos **are** my brothers.
   *(Marcos dan Carlos adalah saudara laki-laki saya.)*

9. We **are** in the coffee bar.
   *(Kami di kedai kopi.)*

10. They **are** in a restaurant.
    *(Mereka di restoran.)*

## PART 5: Grammar Correction
### ✏️ Koreksi Tata Bahasa

Put a check (✓) next to the **CORRECT** sentence:
*(Beri tanda centang pada kalimat yang BENAR)*

1. ✅ **I'm a doctor.** *(Saya seorang dokter.)*
   ❌ I'm doctor.
   > **Why?** Jobs need an article (a/an). *(Mengapa? Pekerjaan membutuhkan kata sandang 'a/an'.)*

2. ❌ I have twenty-nine years old.
   ✅ **I am twenty-nine years old.** *(Saya berumur 29 tahun.)*
   > **Why?** Use "to be" for age, not "have". *(Mengapa? Gunakan "to be" untuk umur, bukan "have".)*

3. ❌ I no married.
   ✅ **I'm not married.** *(Saya tidak menikah.)*
   > **Why?** Negative "to be" is "am not", not "no". *(Mengapa? Bentuk negatif "to be" adalah "am not", bukan "no".)*

4. ✅ **My sister's name is Laura.** *(Nama saudara perempuan saya Laura.)*
   ❌ My sisters name is Laura.
   > **Why?** Use **'s** for possession. *(Mengapa? Gunakan **'s** untuk kepemilikan.)*

5. ❌ She married.
   ✅ **She's married.** *(Dia menikah.)*
   > **Why?** Missing verb "is". *(Mengapa? Kurang kata kerja "is".)*

6. ✅ **I'm an uncle.** *(Saya seorang paman.)*
   ❌ I'm a uncle.
   > **Why?** Use **an** before vowels (u). *(Mengapa? Gunakan **an** sebelum huruf vokal.)*

7. ❌ I have two brother.
   ✅ **I have two brothers.** *(Saya punya dua saudara laki-laki.)*
   > **Why?** Plural nouns add **s**. *(Mengapa? Kata benda jamak menambahkan **s**.)*

8. ❌ Patrick's my sister son's.
   ✅ **Patrick's my sister's son.** *(Patrick adalah anak saudara perempuan saya.)*
   > **Why?** Possession chain: Sister's son. *(Mengapa? Rantai kepemilikan: Anak (dari) saudara perempuan.)*

```
I have classes in English at La Guardia Community College. I'm in a class

New York is very big and very exciting, but very expensive. The subway
isn't hard to use and it's cheap. It's very cold now, but Central Park is

Write to me soon.
Love,
Dorita
```

## PART 9: Ordering Food - Conversations
### 🗣️ Memesan Makanan - Percakapan

### Asking About Prices (Menanyakan Harga)

**Question:** How much is...? *(Berapa harga...?)*

**Examples:**

**Q:** How much is a hamburger and fries?
*(Berapa harga hamburger dan kentang goreng?)*
**A:** Four dollars and seventy-five cents.
*(Empat dolar tujuh puluh lima sen.)*

**Q:** How much is a hamburger and fries and an orange juice?
*(Berapa harga hamburger dan kentang goreng dan jus jeruk?)*
**A:** Six twenty-five.
*(Enam dolar dua puluh lima sen.)*

> [!TIP]
> **Roleplay Tip:** Practice with a friend. One person is the server (A), and the other is the customer (B). Switch roles!
> *(Tip Bermain Peran: Berlatihlah dengan teman. Satu orang menjadi pelayan (A), dan yang lain menjadi pelanggan (B). Tukar peran!)*

> [!NOTE]
> **Culture Note (USA):** In the US, people usually give a **tip** (extra money) to waiters/waitresses (15-20%).
> *(Catatan Budaya (AS): Di AS, orang biasanya memberikan **tip** (uang tambahan) kepada pelayan (15-20%).)*

## Grammar Summary
### 📖 Ringkasan Tata Bahasa

### 1. Verb "TO BE" - Present Tense

#### ✅ Positive:
```
I am / I'm
You are / You're
He/She/It is / He's/She's/It's
We are / We're
They are / They're
```

#### ❌ Negative:
```
I'm not
You aren't / You're not
He/She/It isn't / He's/She's/It's not
We aren't / We're not
They aren't / They're not
```

#### ❓ Questions:
```
Am I...?
Are you...?
Is he/she/it...?
Are we...?
Are they...?
```

---

### 2. Possessive 'S

- **Contraction:** She's = She is *(Dia adalah)*
- **Possession:** Patrick's wife *(Istri Patrick)*

---

### 3. Articles

- Use **"a"** before consonant sounds: **a** teacher, **a** student
- Use **"an"** before vowel sounds: **an** uncle, **an** apartment

---

### 4. Question Formation

| Question Word | Translation | Example |
